Shree Ashtavinayak Ganpati 🙏


Ganesha is the most worshipped deity in India. Maharashtra has a special aura due to the holy Ashtavinayak temples. The Sanskrit word “Ashtavinayak” means “eight Ganeshas”. These eight temples are located in different places, and it is believed that all these Ganeshas are “swayambhu” or self-born. And all these Ganesha idols are “awakened”, meaning they fulfill the desires of the devotees. Each of these Ganeshas is known by different names and each name has a story associated with it.

Mayureshwar Temple – Morgaon


Among the Ashtavinayak temples, the first temple is Moreshwar. The Ashtavinayak pilgrimage begins from here. The Moreshwar temple is located about 55 kilometers from Pune, and the road leading to the temple is excellent. The temple is 50 feet tall and very beautiful. It has entrances in all four directions, but the main entrance faces north. In the temple premises, there are two tall lamp towers (Deepstambhas). Directly in front of the temple there is a large statue of a mouse (Mushak), the vehicle of Lord Ganesha. Also, outside the temple facing the Ganesha idol, there is a large black stone statue of Nandi. This is unusual because Nandi is normally placed in front of Lord Shiva temples. In the temple, worship is performed three times a day: at 7:00 AM, 12:00 PM, and 8:00 PM. During special festivals such as Ganesh Jayanti (Magh Shukla Chaturthi), Ganesh Chaturthi (Bhadrapada Shukla Chaturthi), and every Shukla Chaturthi of the month, the temple becomes very crowded. Devotees from all over the world visit during this time for darshan. Festivals such as Somvati Amavasya and Vijayadashami are also celebrated here.

Siddhivinayak Temple – Siddhatek


Siddhivinayak, or Siddhatek village, is located in Karjat taluka of Ahmednagar district. This temple is the second temple in the Ashtavinayak pilgrimage. It is called Siddhivinayak because it is believed that Lord Vishnu attained Siddhi here. It is believed that this Ganesha fulfills the wishes of devotees, which is why he is called Siddhivinayak. The Siddhivinayak temple is located on the banks of the Bhima River, about 200 kilometers from Pune. The temple is situated on a small hill called Siddhatek. Devotees perform seven circumambulations of this hill to please Lord Ganesha. The idol in the temple is three feet tall and is the only idol among the Ashtavinayak temples with a right-curved trunk, which is a unique feature. In the 18th century, Queen Ahilyabai Holkar renovated and rebuilt the present temple. Other structures in the temple premises were built later by different people. This north-facing temple is made of black stone. The sanctum of the temple is 10 feet wide and 15 feet high. The main festivals celebrated here are Ganesh Chaturthi in the month of Bhadrapada and Ganesh Jayanti in the month of Magh.

Ballaleshwar Temple – Pali


The Ballaleshwar temple is located in Raigad district of Maharashtra, about 28 kilometers from Roha. The temple is situated between the Amba River and the famous Sarasgad Fort. The distance from Pune to Ballaleshwar temple is about 200 kilometers. The original wooden temple was renovated, and the present stone temple was built in 1780. The temple faces east and is designed so that the rays of the rising sun fall directly on the idol of Lord Ganesha. There is a huge bell in the temple courtyard. This bell was offered by Chimaji Appa, the younger brother of Peshwa Bajirao I, after his victory over the Portuguese. Two main festivals are celebrated here: Ganesh Chaturthi in the month of Bhadrapada and Ganesh Jayanti in the month of Magh. During this time, many religious celebrations take place.

Varadvinayak Temple – Mahad


The idol in the Varadvinayak temple is also Swayambhu, meaning it is self-manifested. This idol was found in a nearby pond in the year 1690. The temple was later constructed in 1725 by Subhedar Ramji Mahadev Biwalkar at the place where the idol was established. The idol in the temple faces east. Four elephant statues are installed in the four directions around the temple. An eternal lamp has been burning continuously in this temple since 1892. Devotees from all over the world come to visit Varadvinayak. In this temple, devotees are allowed to enter the sanctum and perform worship. Varadvinayak is located at Mahad in Khalapur taluka of Raigad district in Maharashtra. The temple is well connected by road to all major cities in Maharashtra. Although the temple remains crowded throughout the year, the number of devotees increases significantly during the month of Magh, especially on Chaturthi.

Chintamani Temple – Theur


The Shri Chintamani temple, located about 25 kilometers from Pune, is the largest and one of the most famous temples among the Ashtavinayak temples. The temple has existed since ancient times. The present structure was built by Morya Gosavi. Peshwa Madhavrao was a devoted follower of Chintamani Ganesha and used to visit this temple regularly. During his final days, he stayed in this temple and passed away here. The temple is very large and built of stone. In the temple courtyard, Madhavrao Peshwa built a wooden assembly hall. The idol in the temple is Swayambhu (self-manifested), and except for the head, the other body parts are not clearly visible. The idol has diamonds embedded in its eyes. Three main festivals are celebrated here. The first is Ganesh Chaturthi in the month of Bhadrapada, during which a large fair is held. Ganesh Jayanti is celebrated in the month of Magh as the birth anniversary of Lord Ganesha. On the Ashtami of the Kartik month, Rama-Madhav Punyotsav is celebrated in memory of Madhavrao Peshwa and Sati Ramabai.

Girijatmaj Temple – Lenyadri


The Girijatmaj temple is also known as Lenyadri. It is located in the Ganesh caves in Junnar taluka of Pune district. The temple is about 95 kilometers from Pune. There are a total of 30 caves in this complex, and the Girijatmaj temple is located in the 7th cave. These caves are about 100 feet above ground level. The temple is approximately 1700 years old. Devotees have to climb about 300 steps to reach the temple for darshan. The temple is remarkable from the perspective of sculpture and architecture. It is very simple and natural in appearance. Festivals are celebrated here during the months of Bhadrapada and Magh. Apart from this, the temple is specially decorated and special prayers are offered on every Chaturthi of the month.

Vighneshwar Temple – Ozar


The Vighneshwar temple at Ozar is located about 85 kilometers from Pune. It is situated on the banks of the Kukadi River in Junnar taluka of Pune district along the Pune–Nashik road. Chimaji Appa, the brother of Peshwa Bajirao I, renovated the temple and covered the temple spire with gold. The temple complex is surrounded by walls on all sides and has a large entrance gate. There are two tall lamp towers (Deepstambhas) in the temple courtyard. The main idol of Lord Ganesha faces east. Diamonds are embedded in the forehead and navel of the idol, and rubies are set in the eyes. Festivals are celebrated here during the months of Bhadrapada and Magh. Special celebrations and prayers are also performed on every Chaturthi of the month. On Kartik Purnima, both lamp towers are illuminated with hundreds of lamps.

Mahaganapati Temple – Ranjangaon


The Shri Mahaganapati temple is located at Ranjangaon on the Pune–Ahmednagar highway, about 50 kilometers from Pune. The present temple was built by the Peshwas. This east-facing temple has a beautiful and grand entrance gate. The structure of the temple is designed so that during Dakshinayan, the rays of the rising sun fall directly on the idol of Lord Ganesha. Four main festivals are celebrated at the Mahaganapati temple: Bhadrapada Chaturthi, Magh Chaturthi, the month of Shravan, and the month of Phalgun. In addition, special prayers are performed on every Chaturthi of the month.
